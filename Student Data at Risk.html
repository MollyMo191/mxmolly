Student Data at Risk


I designed several magazine spreads that were inspired by my participation in the Redbirds for Privacy student movement at Illinois State University, which gained statewide attention at the height of the 2020 COVID pandemic. The movement was in opposition to the usage of ProctorTrack by the university, an AI-based proctoring software that was implemented during remote learning, and had a significant negative effect on students.

The article used in these spreads is “Poor Security at Online Proctoring Company May Have Put Student Data at Risk,” published on Consumer Reports by writer Thomas Germain in late 2020.

I featured typographic experimentation with photographs of cameras, as well as illustrations of eyes to convey the message of the article—that the issue of student privacy being infringed upon by AI-based proctoring software also put the spotlight on students' rights in higher education.
