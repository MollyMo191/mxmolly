Friday Again


Friday Again is an interactive and introspective poster design project. The player is tasked with organizing the weekend schedule of a ghost by physically moving tasks from a bank of words over to a board organized chronologically into comic panels, which are divvied up by blocks of time across a three-day weekend.

To accomplish the movement and personality of the character, I photographed myself in many different scenarios, which I then used to create the ghost—a gender-neutral character that anyone can relate to. I organized the board in a comic book style to convey the passage of time and narrative progression.

Initially I had wanted this project to be a reflection on how we percieve free time, however it evolved into an interesting study on what tasks get pushed to the backburner when we’re on a time crunch. Most players forfeited sleeping, eating, and relaxation to make room for other work and school-oriented tasks, which were nearly always regarded as more important within the game.
